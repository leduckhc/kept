Grouped Threads — Ring-Based Variants

Constraint: colored ring already signals receiving account (blue = work, green = personal, etc.).
How do we signal "this is a group of N threads" using the ring without conflicting?

Variant A
Segmented ring — unread ratio as arc fill
The account ring becomes a progress indicator: filled arc = unread proportion. A group with 3/4 unread shows 75% filled arc in the account color. Single threads show full ring (as today). Signals both "which account" and "how much is unread" in one element.

Dashed ring = group, solid ring = individual
Groups use a dashed ring in the account color. Individuals keep solid ring. Instantly distinguishes "one thread" from "multiple threads" at a glance. The dash pattern itself communicates "collection." Simple CSS change, no SVG needed.

Decision notes
A — Segmented arc: Most info-dense. Shows account color + unread ratio in one ring. But adds visual complexity — could feel "app-y" or fitness-tracker-ish.
B — Dashed ring: Simplest implementation (CSS only). Dashed = group is intuitive (like a "collection" boundary). Clean, minimal. But dashes at small size may look noisy.
C — Double ring: Subtle "bullseye" effect. Gentle signal, doesn't change the primary ring. But may be too subtle — easy to miss at a glance.
D — Ring + count bubble: Keeps the solid ring untouched, adds iOS-style count badge sitting on it. Most familiar pattern (notifications). Clear, immediate, no ambiguity.

Recommendation: D — it's the only one that doesn't modify the ring itself (which already has account-color semantics). The count bubble is additive, not conflicting. Everyone already knows what a notification badge means.
