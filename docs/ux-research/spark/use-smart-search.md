# Use Smart Search

Source: https://sparkmailapp.com/help/manage-your-inbox/use-smart-search
Scraped: 2026-05-31T10:50:49Z

---

With Spark, you can quickly find any email using its smart search. Spark can filter emails by content, date, sender or recipient, attachments, etc.

**Note:**For Gmail accounts, Spark searches through all folders except Trash and Spam. For non-Gmail accounts, Spark looks for emails in Inbox and Archive unless you specify the folder name in your search query.

[Mac](#) [Windows](#) [Android](#) [iOS](#)

1. Open Spark and click the search icon at the top right, or press **Cmd ⌘**+**F**(please note, this shortcut activates search through the body of the currently opened email, and through your Inbox if no email is currently opened).
2. Type your query in the search field and press **Enter**.

You can write a word or phrase or specify the needed sender, date (except for the exact dates such as 06.07.2020, September 5th, etc.), and so on. Spark understands natural language; you can type, “from: Amazon last month” to see how it works.

3. If you have multiple accounts, you can choose a particular one to search through. After entering your query, click on the arrow buttons and select the account you wish. By default, Spark searches through all your accounts.

## Search emails by date

Include one of the following phrases in your search query: “Last week,” “today, " “yesterday,” “this week,” “this month,” “last two weeks,” “last 2 weeks,” “last month,” or “last two months.” For example, you can type, “report last month.”

## Search emails with attachments

You can find emails with attached images, PDFs, and documents. Start typing "has:" for Spark Desktop and "has" for Spark on mobile devices and choose the keyword from the list.

Spark shows the emails with highlighted attachments.

## Search emails by subject

Type “subject:” in the search field and write a word or phrase from a subject line.

## Search emails in specific folders

Type “in:” for Spark Desktop and "in" for Spark on mobile devices in the search field and enter the name of the folder you want to search through (e.g. Drafts, Sent, Archive, or a custom folder you created). Add a word or phrase you are looking for.

## Search emails by the domain name

[Mac](#) [Windows](#) [Android](#) [iOS](#)

Type "from:" and the domain name in the search field. For example, when you enter "from: @readdle.com, " Spark finds all messages from people with @readdle.com emails. You can also include multiple domain names in your search query (e.g. "from: @readdle.com, from: @apple.com ").

## Other search operators

You can type these words in the search field to find specific emails:

[Mac](#) [Windows](#) [Android](#) [iOS](#)

* is: Pinned.
* is: Unread — to search through emails you haven't read yet.

You can combine different words and search operators to find any needed emails faster. For example, “report has: PDF from: Ann last month.”
